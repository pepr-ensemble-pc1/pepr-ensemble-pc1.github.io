## Thesis Title

Analyse et conception des interactions et des collaborations nécessaires pour assister la mobilité piétonne

## Abstract

Mobility (walking) is an essential part of everyday life. However, it can be difficult for some people, particularly those with intellectual disabilities. There are around 900,000 people with intellectual disabilities in France, who experience difficulties with intellectual and adaptive functions. Assistive tools such as Google Maps can be useful, but are often too complex and do not provide personalised assistance. In order to help people with intellectual disabilities with their mobility, and more specifically with wayfinding, this thesis aims to study solutions for collaboration with one or more remote persons. This collaboration could prove particularly useful in unexpected situations encountered by people with intellectual disabilities (e.g. a cancelled bus or an inaccessible route). The carer, whether professional or not, must be able to understand where the person in difficulty is and help them either find a new route or put them in touch with other people. Collaboration will therefore take place between carers and with the person with an intellectual disability in order to involve them in finding a solution. The aim is to assess the acceptance and effectiveness of solutions according to profiles and configurations.

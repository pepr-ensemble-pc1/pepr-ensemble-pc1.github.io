## Thesis Title

Collaboration in Hybrid Environments:co-defining collaborative spatial layouts

## Abstract

My research focuses on remote collaboration between participants interacting in heterogeneous environments. Heterogeneous environments are made of a diversity of input and output modalities ranging from desktop computers to advanced mixed reality devices. Heterogeneous environments allow the distribution of content and data among devices, according to each person's role. Such environments can be distributed in different sites. However, distance and differences between heterogeneous environments may hinder collaboration.
To address these barriers, we first focused on the identification and characterization of the asymmetries that can arise between users’ interactions across collaborative heterogeneous systems. To this end, we conducted a systematic literature review of asymmetric collaborative systems, coding their properties, including the interaction spaces, their input and output modalities, and shared feedback. This work led to the definition of the dimensions of asymmetrical collaboration. 
Second, we are now exploring the development of interaction techniques that promote remote workspace awareness and effective means of communication despite asymmetries between environments. To this end, we are exploring the use of a World-in-Miniature (WIM) in Augmented Reality (AR) representing the remote heterogeneous environment. This WIM provides awareness cues about the remote collaborators’ activities (cone of vision, device used, hands gestures, positions, alerts) and also constitutes a communication interface (screen-sharing, remote pointing). The AR headset used to display the WIM allows the user to keep their hands free and easily access their own devices.

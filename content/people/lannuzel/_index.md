## Thesis Title

Vers l’amélioration de l’intelligence collective en réalité virtuelle

## Abstract

My doctoral research focuses on collective intelligence in collaborative virtual reality environments. The objective is to better understand how small teams coordinate, communicate, and perform complex tasks when working together in immersive digital spaces, particularly in professional and educational contexts.

Rather than viewing team performance only through final outcomes, the thesis adopts a process-oriented perspective, examining how collective intelligence emerges dynamically through interaction. Virtual reality is used as a controlled yet ecologically valid medium, enabling teams to share the same space, manipulate common objects, and collaborate under clearly defined constraints.

The research combines experimental VR scenarios with systematic observation of team behavior, allowing the study of coordination, participation, and collaboration patterns during task execution. These observations are then used to model team functioning at a higher level, linking interaction processes to overall effectiveness.

A broader goal of the thesis is to contribute to the development of computational and theoretical frameworks for collective intelligence that are applicable to real-world collaborative work. By bridging cognitive science, human–computer interaction, and immersive technologies, this work aims to inform both the scientific understanding of teamwork and the design of future collaborative VR systems.

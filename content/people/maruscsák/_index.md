## Thesis Title

InstaBeam: Instant Beaming into Immersive Viewing Environments

## Abstract

In the context of fashion design, translating design sketches to sewable garments relies on
patternmakers, who typically use specialized Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software. Our goal is to automate the process empowering designers to go directly from sketch to sewing
patterns. We first conducted a formative study comprising story-interviews with designers and in-situ observations to identify design opportunities for such a system. The study revealed that designers require sketching interface. We then conducted an elicitation study to understand how garment editing commands are communicated using sketches. Through qualitative analysis of 850 sketch marks, we derived design guidelines towards the development of intelligent sketch-based interactive systems. Building on our design guidelines, we are developing a sketch-based interactive system for digital fashion design.

## Thesis Title

Conception d’outils pour la collaboration lors de travaux pratiques en réalité virtuelle

## Abstract

This PhD project focuses on human–computer interaction, education, and remote collaboration, with the goal of exploring how immersive technologies can support collaborative learning. Because virtual environments cannot fully replicate real-world experiences, it is essential to consider both human activity and the specific capabilities and limitations of technology when designing collaborative systems. As a result, this research goes beyond simply transferring face-to-face educational practices into virtual settings. Instead, it seeks to identify and design new communication styles and collaboration strategies that are better suited to virtual education. Especially, this research focus on the impact of immersive technologies on collaborative behaviours.

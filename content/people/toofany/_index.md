## Thesis Title

Social Haptics : Study of Haptic Feedback for Promoting Positive Social Interactions in Virtual Reality

## Abstract

The general context of the PhD relates to “shared virtual environments”, in which multiple
users can interact by means of immersive technologies. An economic craze has recently
developed on this topic, especially with the buzz around "metaverses", these massively shared virtual worlds accessible using Virtual Reality (VR) headsets. Beyond entertainment and video games, the applications of shared virtual environments are varied and numerous, including:
education, training, cultural heritage, medicine, etc.
The objective of this PhD thesis is to study the role and interest of the haptic modality in
collaborative scenarios for promoting positive social interactions in Virtual Reality. More precisely, the objective is to :
1) understand the role of haptic modality in collaborative scenarios, i.e. the identification
of meaningful haptic cues and percepts for supporting group interactions (perception/cognitive science) and
2) design, implement and evaluate new haptic devices (hardware & software) simulating
tactile/force feedback to one or multiple users.
For this aim, various kinds of haptic technologies will be considered, including wearable
haptic devices, non-contact haptic stimulation (ultrasound), vibratory actuators, encountered-type haptics, pseudo-haptics, tangible elements, etc. But also different part of the body will be concerned for the touch (arm, shoulder, back, etc.). The challenges are both hardware and software: The device should be robust and flexible enough to support a wide range of scenarios. The successful candidate will explore the design space offered by such haptic displays and progressively determine which modalities are the most suitable for social interactions in VR, in order to either transmit positive interactions from participants, promote or moderate interventions, etc.

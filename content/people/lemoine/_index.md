## Thesis Title

PARMI – Personalised and Accessible extended Reality Musical Instruments for Practitioners-Patients Collaboration in Music Therapy

## Abstract

My thesis aims to investigate the co-design of personalized, accessible, and collaborative virtual musical instruments in extended reality that would enable the practitioners to adapt the instruments and guide and support their autistic children patients during collective music-making sessions. Our goal is to foster synchronous, asymmetric and heterogeneous collaboration among users with varied psycho-cognitive profiles, ensuring that musical creation is a shared experience between the therapist and the child. A multidisciplinary approach will be adopted combining approaches from computer science (human-computer interaction, extended reality) and approaches inspired from human sciences (psychology, cognitive ergonomics) to design such immersive interfaces for collective musical expression.

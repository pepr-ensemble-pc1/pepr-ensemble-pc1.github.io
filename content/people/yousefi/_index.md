## Thesis Title

Exploration de la tangibilisation de la présence socio-émotionnelle et de son influence sur les interactions et l’expérience d’apprentissage au cours de sessions d’apprentissage/travail collaboratif

## Abstract

Empirical research has shown that collaboration involves an intense process of negotiation, conflicting points of view, constant interaction with others and shared work artefacts. This process presents socio-emotional challenges that require the individuals to have regulatory skills. This regulation covers a broad process in which cognition, emotion, motivation and behavior are adapted according to the objectives, goals, interactions and norms of the individuals and the group. 
While collaborative processes require operational synchronization and coordination, they also need an awareness of the emotional state of group members to ensure a positive and motivating working climate. However, it is very common for members of a group not to be trained in emotional awareness. 
To encourage optimal collaboration, it is crucial to make both learners and their teachers aware of the importance of emotional presence in collaborative dynamics. In this context, training them to be more aware of the affects of the individuals making up the group and their influence on collaborative dynamics, therefore appears strategic. 
Using a design-based research methodology, the aim of this project is to study the expressions, sharing and influences of socio-emotional presence in a collaborative context, whether or not supported by a tangible interface embodying that presence.

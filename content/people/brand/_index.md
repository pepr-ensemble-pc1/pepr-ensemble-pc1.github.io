## Thesis Title

COLLABORATION TEMPS RÉEL DANS DES ESPACES HYBRIDES MELANT ENVIRONNEMENTS VIRTUELS ET INTERFACES TANGIBLES

## Abstract

My PhD focuses on the design and evaluation of immersive systems for asymmetric remote collaboration, in contexts involving complex and critical tasks (e.g., a remote expert on Earth guiding an isolated operator on Mars). It is situated within the fields of Human-Computer Interaction and Virtual Reality.

The main objective is to investigate how different interaction modalities and immersive display setups can support the modification of structured procedures.
Following a user-centered approach, this thesis proposes both situated and non-situated interfaces in VR that enable visualization and modification of a procedure.

In this thesis, we compare three interaction modalities (controller-based interaction, gesture-based interaction, and tangible object manipulation on an interactive table) combined with two visualization modalities (situated and non-situated) for modifying complex procedures in a virtual reality environment (within a CAVE).
Through controlled experimental protocols, this work aims to empirically assess how these combinations influence task performance, cognitive workload, and user experience.

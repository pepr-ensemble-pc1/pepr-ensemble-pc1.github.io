## Thesis Title

Étude des interactions haptiques interpersonnelles entre avatars virtuels

## Abstract

The ANR MASTERS project (2023–2027) aims to design innovative pedagogical methods for law enforcement officers, firefighters, and healthcare professionals. These methods seek to help trainees maintain effective self-control in all circumstances, including situations involving high levels of constraint and intense stress (of psychological, social, or other origins) associated with challenging and dynamic contexts.

Within this project, the contribution of our teams is to design and develop virtual environments, multisensory interfaces, and dynamic models that enable realistic training and situational immersion tailored to these specific contexts.

The objective of this PhD project is, in a first phase, to implement within the platform an approach that enables the dynamic handling of physical contacts in virtual environments and their restitution to learners through haptic devices, such as vests or full-body suits. To achieve this, a modular approach is proposed. This approach consists in considering each avatar or virtual object as an independent elementary entity, while the connections between entities are treated as white-box components capable of communicating with the digital models of the involved entities.

In a second phase, the project aims to investigate the impact of these physical interactions on learners’ situational experience. More specifically, building on our model of avatar fidelity in immersive environments and on our previous studies on haptic communication, we plan to examine the contribution of the haptic modality, in addition to the visual modality, to the sense of presence and the sense of co-presence. This work will constitute the first step toward the development of the concept of the multisensory avatar.

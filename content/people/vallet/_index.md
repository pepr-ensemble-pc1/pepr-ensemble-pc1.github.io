## Thesis Title

Performances Artistiques Immersives Collaboratives 

## Abstract

My thesis consists of designing ways for artists to collaborate during immersive artistic performances, or between artists and audiences. This may involve creating new scenographies for immersive scenes that facilitate a sense of co-presence (the feeling of sharing a virtual environment with someone) among the audience in a shared space. This is the case in my first work on a scenography using a method of locomotion known as “spatial compartmentalization,” which allows the audience to explore a performance rather than watch it passively. The physical space is compartmentalized by virtual zones in which there is a viewpoint on a virtual environment larger than the physical space. Access to these zones is achieved by walking, which ensures spatial synchronization between all members of the audience. But my subject also allows me to work on methods of asymmetric (or non-asymmetric) and asynchronous (or non-asynchronous) interaction between the audience and artists, as well as the creation of new interaction devices to give artists more expressiveness during performances.

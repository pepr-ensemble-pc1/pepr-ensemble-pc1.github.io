## Thesis Title

Détection des (inter)actions d'un opérateur distant assisté en Réalité Augmentée et collaborant avec un expert local en Réalité Virtuelle pour de l’assistance en milieux isolés : application à la médecine spatiale

## Abstract

My thesis aims to detect and recognise the (inter)actions performed by an isolated operator during an Augmented Reality Guided Procedure using various forms of input. 
These inputs range from high level semantic information about the procedure and scene to low level system data collected by sensors.
The semantic information will be provided through an environmental knowledge and human activity representation meta-model (Application Domain Model), which can provide the states, attributes and current possible actions to be performed by the isolated operator.
Additional data is collected through devices such as a Kinect, connected objects, and infrared cameras, while information about the virtual environment is gathered by the underlying simulation platform.

In order to detect and recognise the (inter)actions performed, this information is then provided to Human Activity Recognition models in order to deduce the current activity performed within the context.

The application of this topic is that of a medical procedure in an isolated and potentially asynchronous space, where an operator (ranging from an astronaut on Mars, to a soldier at sea), guided by AR information, is collaborating with a medical expert in a virtual representation of the isolated environment.

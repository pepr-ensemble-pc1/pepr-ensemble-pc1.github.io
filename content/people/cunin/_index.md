## Thesis Title

Édition et synchronisation du Jumeau Numérique en réalité virtuelle et réalité augmentée pour l’usine du futur

## Abstract

This PhD thesis focuses on the 3D authoring of a digital twin in augmented reality and virtual reality for the industry of the future. This research project is part of a national project: 5G Metaverse. The authoring task can be divided into three subtasks: (1) the selection to define the subparts of the 3D models, (2) the positioning of the segmented parts to match the physical twin, and (3) the behavior creation to define movements of parts. Three main challenges have been found to enable end-users, such as operators, technicians, or engineers, to easily author 3D models: (1) asymmetrical synchronous collaboration between an in-situ worker and a remote expert, (2) usability for end-users as they are not 3D authoring experts, and (3) cross-platform ability to suit remote immersive collaboration. Our first study will compare three different interaction techniques (1) on a desktop or (2) in an immersive environment using a virtual reality headset, to find the most suitable technique for a specified platform for a selection task. The next study will focus on collaborative authoring using the best techniques identified in the first study.

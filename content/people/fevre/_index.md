## Thesis Title

PhyDiViews – Collaboration in Hybrid Environments (AR, VR, desktop): Multi-Views with Different Physicalities and Dimensions

## Abstract

In recent years, particularly following the COVID-19 pandemic and the development of mixed reality technologies, there has been an increase in research projects focusing on remote collaboration systems. In many collaborative scenarios, remote participants must explore multiple points of view to better understand the environment and address complex problems. This thesis aims to enhance remote collaboration by investigating methods for sharing and synchronising points of view across multiple views with different physicalities and dimensions. It also aims to evaluate the impacts of transitions between different views on users’ cognitive load and spatial awareness.
To achieve this, we first introduce a notation, PV*, which describes the coupling and control of points of view in remote collaboration systems, enabling a clearer understanding of their relationships. Using the PV*, we will then explore new solutions for managing and sharing multiple points of view.
